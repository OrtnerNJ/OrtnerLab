Molecular mechanisms to translational insight

ORTNER Lab

Voltage-gated Calcium Channel Signaling, Variants & Precision Medicine

We study how voltage-gated calcium channels shape cellular signaling, how disease-associated variants alter function, and how mechanistic insight can support precision medicine approaches across neurological and systemic disease.

Explore our research Join the lab

Research at a glance

Signaling: understanding channel-dependent cellular communication.

Variants: defining how genetic changes reshape channel behavior.

Precision Medicine: linking molecular mechanisms to therapeutic opportunity.

Research Focus

Our work connects basic ion channel biology with disease mechanisms and translational questions. We aim to build a collaborative and mechanistic understanding of how calcium channel dysfunction contributes to human disease.

Channel Signaling

We investigate how voltage-gated calcium channels regulate signaling pathways, excitability, and cellular function across physiological contexts.

Disease Variants

We study the functional consequences of channel variants and how altered biophysical properties contribute to disease phenotypes.

Precision Medicine

We connect molecular and genetic findings to patient-relevant questions, with the goal of supporting mechanism-based therapeutic strategies.
